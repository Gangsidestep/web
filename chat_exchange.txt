The most common D&O claim in Switzerland

👉 Social security (AVS/AI/APG) and tax liability of directors

Under Swiss law, directors (or board members of a cooperative) can be personally liable if the organisation fails to pay certain obligations.

The most frequent cases involve:

-
AVS/AI/APG contributions

-
withholding taxes

-
social security deductions from salaries

-
sometimes VAT

If the company becomes insolvent, authorities may claim the unpaid amounts directly from directors.

This is based mainly on:

-
Art. 52 AVS law (LAVS)

-
Art. 754 Swiss Code of Obligations (director liability).

---

It usually happens in this scenario:

-
Organisation has financial difficulties.

-
It delays payment of:

-
social security contributions

-
taxes.

-
Insolvency occurs.

-
Authorities investigate whether the board acted with due diligence.

-
If not, directors become personally liable.

Even non-profit boards are exposed.
